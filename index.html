Backstory & Lore
Raventongue

God of the lost, forgotten, and unwanted. Patron of orphans and thieves. His holy symbol is a rapier with a winged, swept hilt. His people on the Bound Isles do not ask where you came from.

Ember (she/her)

Ember was the last child born to the Light Singers — a clan of fetchling nomads who spent generations traveling west across the Plane of Shadow, forever following the eternally setting sun. The winter Ember was born, every woman in the clan became barren on the same night. The elders knew the prophecy. They did not speak it aloud.

They raised her together instead. Every story the clan had ever carried, every recipe, every song, every memory stretching back further than anyone could trace — poured into her. Ember became a living archive. The last ember of everything the Light Singers had ever been.

She was given clan tattoos as she grew. Deliberate. Elegant. Ceremonial. The markings of someone who knew exactly who they were.

When Ember came of age, the clan stopped walking. They had arrived. A crumbling temple — half consumed by shadow, half blazing with the light of the setting sun. Each elder told her one final story. A story of calamity and renewal. She cannot remember the words. She feels them still.

The last thing Ember saw of home was the faces of everyone she had ever loved, slowly consumed by darkness blacker than death as the portal opened beneath her feet.

The Pit

The vampire lords did not take the Light Singers for food. They took them for sport.

The clan was separated, imprisoned, and introduced to the arena one threshold at a time. The fights began with beasts. Then monsters. Then rival clans. Each time Ember survived, she was rewarded — elixirs that produced euphoria unlike anything she had words for. Warmth. Relief. The chemical sensation of having done something right.

She began to associate the arena with that feeling. The conditioning was patient and thorough.

By the time they put her against members of her own clan, the threshold had already been crossed so many times it barely registered. By the time they put her against her family, her hands were already steady.

Her spouse was last. She does not remember how long the fight lasted. She remembers the expression on their face at the end. Not hatred. Not fear.

Forgiveness.

She remembers what the elixir felt like afterward. That is the part that has no shape. That is the wound underneath all the other wounds — the one the tattoos were never going to cover.

The Name

When it was over, the Archlord looked at what remained of Ember and gave it a name. Nix. A curse word in his mother tongue. Then he pressed a drop of his own blood to their lips, spoke three bindings into it, and cast them into the void between planes.

An experiment. What happens when you take everything from someone — and then take even more?

Nix kept the name. They don't fully know why. Somewhere underneath the chaos, underneath the voices and the conditioning and the arguments, something kept it on purpose. Something that hasn't announced itself as a reason. Something patient and small and not entirely extinguished.

Ember knows why.

Nix tells her to shut up about it.

The Three Bindings
Binding I You cannot die by your own hand.

The blood crystallizes in Nix's veins if they attempt self-destruction — paralyzing, agonizing, absolute. This is not mercy. The experiment is not finished. Reckless choices are permitted. Suicide is not.

Binding II You cannot be hidden from him.

The Archlord always knows where Nix is. He has not come to collect them yet. This is its own kind of horror — being watched by something that hasn't decided what to do with you.

Binding III You hear them all.

The Archlord's blood carries the essence of everyone whose blood has ever mingled with his. Nix hears their voices. All of them. Constantly. Ember is the one Nix gave a name to. Ember is the one Nix argues with out loud. The others have not been named.

Nix

Nix emerged from the void onto the Bound Isles. Found Raventongue's people. Survived. Functioned. After a fashion.

The guild came later — not by plan. Nix happened to see a member of the Blightfather's clergy speaking to a Delvers Guild recruiter. The Blightfather is known to abhor vampires. Something steered Nix toward that conversation without announcing itself as a reason. Nix signed the contract. They tell themselves it was practical.

Ember hasn't said anything about it. Which is unusual.

The guild quartermaster has an informal chalk mark system for when Nix comes in. Everyone is suddenly very busy with paperwork. It's the stillness, mostly. The way Nix goes completely, perfectly calm at the exact moment when a reasonable person would not be calm. The slight tilt of the head.

That, and the arguments with Ember.

The Ember Problem

Ember is still in there. She remembers everything — every story, every recipe, every face of the clan, every moment in the arena, every reward, every threshold crossed, the expression at the end. She has opinions. She does not stay quiet.

Nix does not open the archive. Nix addresses Ember directly, out loud, when necessary, and considers this perfectly normal. It is not perfectly normal.

Ember is also the loudest voice. She is not the only one.

"SHUT UP EMBER."
"This is not your party, Ember."
"No, Ember. The dagger is not a weapon. I don't care what you think."
"...I know, Ember. I know."
A Note on the Conditioning

Nix uses bombs. Nix has a mutagen. Nix goes very still before violence and very calm during it.

The vampire lords' conditioning did not disappear when Ember became Nix.

It just got a new name.
